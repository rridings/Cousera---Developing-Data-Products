What to pack

Every had trouble packing for trip with an extended stay?


Current weather sites are great for giving current forcecasts. But what if you needed to know how to pack, for an entire month or six months.

That is where Weather Monthly fits in.

Weather Monthly

Using 1981-2010 U.S. Climate Normals from the National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration, Weather Monthly can tell you the average minumum and maximun temperature for a specfic location in a given month. Additionally, Weather Monthly will provide the average snowfall, in case you want to pack your skis.


1981-2010 U.S. Climate Normals

How to use

  1. Identify a location by its longitude and latitude coordinates.
  2. Select a radius in miles to identify a circle around that point.
  3. Select the month you are interest in.

    For example

    John Hopkins Univeristy is located at 39.3289 N, 76.6203 W. Let's draw a 50 mile circle around that point and see what we can expect in January.

Results

John Hopkins University in January

longitude <- -76.6203
latitude <- 39.3289
month <- 'jan'
radius <- 50
df <- data.frame(longitude, latitude, month, radius, stringsAsFactors=FALSE)

w <- weather(df)


Maximum Temperature: 41 F
Minimum Temperature: 24 F
Snowfall: 7.07 inches
